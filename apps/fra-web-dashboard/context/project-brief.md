# Risk Aware Project Brief

## Mission Statement

Deliver an accessible, engaging fraud risk awareness training platform that helps UK government agencies and public sector organisations build a culture of vigilance and compliance.

## Background

The Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 introduced a new "failure to prevent fraud" offence, making organisations liable for fraud committed by employees. This creates an urgent need for comprehensive fraud awareness training across the public sector.

## Objectives

1. **Educate** - Provide clear, actionable information about fraud risks and regulatory requirements
2. **Engage** - Use interactive elements (quizzes, scenarios, polls) to reinforce learning
3. **Empower** - Enable participants to create personal action plans
4. **Evidence** - Issue certificates and track completion for compliance reporting

## Target Users

### Primary
- Trustees and Board Members
- Executive Leadership (CEO, CFO, COO)
- Budget-Holders and Finance Directors

### Secondary
- All staff with financial responsibilities
- Procurement teams
- HR and payroll administrators

## Key Success Metrics

- Workshop completion rate > 90%
- Quiz scores averaging > 80%
- Action plan creation rate > 75%
- User satisfaction rating > 4.0/5.0

## Regulatory Context

- Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023
- UK Bribery Act 2010
- Fraud Act 2006
- Public Sector Fraud Authority guidelines
- Cabinet Office Counter Fraud Standards

## Stakeholders

- Government Digital Service (GDS)
- Public Sector Fraud Authority
- National Audit Office
- Individual agency compliance teams
